The Fundamentals of Xenoblade Chronicles X’s Ground Combat

Xenoblade Chronicles X (XCX) is a real-time RPG with many complex parts to its gameplay. Set on the fictional planet of Mira, you play as a survivor of a spaceship’s crash fighting against aliens who want to wipe out humanity. In the game, you have a broad range of options for how you want to play, divided by your choice of weapons, class, and gear. You will need a Nintendo Wii U and the included GamePad to play the game, though you can use a Wii U Pro Controller as well.

Attacking

The basics of the combat system revolve around two types of attacks: auto-attacks and Arts. When you are locked onto an enemy and in range, you will perform auto-attacks with the weapon you are using at the time with short breaks in between. These auto-attacks are weak, so do not rely on them for damage. When you want to do higher amounts of damage, you use Arts. Arts are special skills that serve different roles depending on the colour of their icon. The following list describes the types of Arts available:

  • Melee: close-ranged attacks, indicated by an orange icon.

  • Ranged: long-ranged attacks, indicated by a yellow icon.

  • Debuff: enemy-weakening skills, indicated by a purple icon.

  • Buff: instant support skills, indicated by a green icon.

  • Aura: long-lasting support skills, indicated by a blue icon.

You can find these Arts at the bottom of the screen and can select which one you want to use by pressing Left or Right on the D-Pad. If you want to use an Art, press the A button over the one you want when it is available.

Weapons

When fighting enemies, you have two weapons that you can attack with. One of them is a melee weapon and the other is a ranged weapon. In a battle, you can only use one at a time, but you can swap your weapon with the X button or by using an Art. The following table describes your options:

Each weapon comes with unique Arts and, Multigun aside, are locked behind their default classes until you reach their maximum level on their best class. For example, you cannot use Dual Guns as a Galactic Knight unless you have reached Rank 10 as a Full Metal Jaguar. The Multigun is an exception because it has no specific class that uses it.

Classes

In XCX, your character has many different playstyles defined by their class. Each class has several differences that change how you play them, like different stat increases and skill options. There are four tiers of classes, with the second and third tiers diverging into different branches. To advance in a branch, you need to raise your Class Rank to Rank 10 by defeating enemies or completing side quests. Each class comes with their own unique Arts and skills, with a certain amount of slots to use them depending on which class is active at the time. Once you have achieved Rank 10 in a fourth-tier class, you can use their weapons regardless of if you remain in that class or not.

Skills

As you advance in your class, you obtain skills at specific ranks. These skills provide improvements to you in many different ways, from stat improvements to conditional buffs to yourself. You can improve them up to five times to boost their effects. You can only use as many as you have slots, so you must be careful when choosing which ones you intend to use.

Tension Points

Tension Points (TP) are to XCX what mana is to most magic-based games. TP is a currency of sorts that you use to perform the following actions:

  • Use Arts that are labeled TP Arts. They use up 1000 TP.

  • Revive fallen party members. This uses up 3000 TP.

  • Activate or replenish Overdrive. This also uses up 3000 TP.

To build up TP, there are many different methods you can use. The first method is by auto-attacking. One Assault Rifle that you get in a side quest called the Ramjet Rifle is good for this. Another method is by using Arts that give you TP under certain conditions, such as Primer or Tornado Blade. By default, your TP caps out at 3000 TP, but you can improve this by using special gear or augments to raise the cap.

Overdrive

After Chapter 5 of the main story, you unlock the Overdrive mechanic. In a fight, you can activate it by pressing A on the button in the centre of your Arts bar when you have 3000 TP. Once you do that, it is active for fifteen seconds by default, which you can extend with the following methods:

  • Using the Hellhound skill, which extends the starting duration by up to 10 seconds.

  • Using Extend Overdrive augments on your armour, which increases the starting duration by up to 25 seconds each.

  • Using auras and buffs during Overdrive.

  • Pressing the Overdrive activation button on the Arts bar with 3000 TP before Overdrive ends.

During this time, all of your Arts recharge quicker than they would normally and a counter appears in the middle of your screen. This counter increases with every damaging Art you use based on how many hits land, capping out at 100 hits. As you raise the counter, your damage and Arts recharge speed increase. Additionally, using specific types of Arts in conjunction with each other gives different effects. See the following list for the combos and their effects:

  • Auras: Extends the timer by 0.1 seconds multiplied by the counter.

  • Buffs: Extends the timer by 0.05 seconds multiplied by the counter.

  • Damaging debuffs: Recharges your TP by 10 multiplied by the counter with each hit.

  • Buffs to auras: Heals you by 10%.

  • Melee to melee: Increases experience gained from the fight by 10%, capping out at 200%.

  • Ranged to ranged: Increases class experience gained from the fight by 10%, capping out at 200%.

  • Buffs to any damaging Arts: Doubles the number of hits added to the counter.

  • Debuffs to any damaging Arts: Boosts damage to specific spots on the enemies’ bodies by 50%.

  • Auras to debuffs: Extends the debuff’s length by 25%.

  • Ranged to melee or melee to ranged: No increases to the counter and no damage boosts. Do not use this combo, for there are no benefits.
